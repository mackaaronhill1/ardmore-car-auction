How to Bid at Our Auctions

A step-by-step guide for new bidders

First Time at an Auction?

Bidding at a car auction can be an exciting experience, especially if it's your first time. At Ardmore Car Auction, we welcome first-time bidders and are here to help you through the process. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to participate in our auctions.

Remember, our staff is always available to answer questions and provide assistance during auction day.

The Bidding Process

1

Registration

Arrive early on auction day to complete your registration. Doors open at 5:30 PM, and registration is available until the auction begins at 7:00 PM.

What You'll Need:

  • Valid government-issued photo ID
  • Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement, etc.)
  • Method of payment for deposit

There is a $25 registration fee, refundable with a vehicle purchase or when you return your bidder card at the end of the auction.

2

Vehicle Inspection

Take time to inspect the vehicles you're interested in. Inspection period runs from 5:30 PM until 6:45 PM.

Tips for Inspection:

  • Bring a flashlight to better see underneath vehicles
  • Check all fluids and look for leaks
  • Examine tires and brake components
  • Look for signs of accident repair or rust
  • Take notes on vehicles you're interested in

Staff members can start vehicles upon request, but test drives are not available.

3

Auction Begins

The auction starts promptly at 7:00 PM. Find a seat in the auction arena and keep your bidder card handy.

The Auction Process:

  • Vehicles are driven or pushed into the auction arena one at a time
  • The auctioneer will announce basic details and starting bid
  • Bidding progresses in increments set by the auctioneer
  • Hold up your bidder card clearly to place a bid
  • The highest bidder wins when the auctioneer calls "Sold!"

Remember, the fast-paced nature of an auction can be overwhelming at first. Watch a few vehicles go through the process before bidding.

4

Winning a Bid

If you're the winning bidder, you'll be directed to the cashier's office to complete initial paperwork.

What Happens Next:

  • Pay a deposit of $500 or 10% of purchase price (whichever is greater)
  • Sign a purchase agreement
  • Receive information about completing payment and pickup

The deposit is non-refundable and will be applied to your total purchase price.

5

Payment & Pickup

Full payment must be made within 48 hours of the auction. Vehicles must be picked up within 3 business days after payment.

Payment Options:

  • Cash
  • Credit Cards (subject to 3% processing fee)
  • Bank Check or Money Order
  • Wire Transfer

Personal checks are not accepted. Vehicles not picked up after 7 days will incur a storage fee of $25 per day.

Tips for Successful Bidding

Set a Budget

Decide on your maximum bid before the auction starts and stick to it. It's easy to get caught up in the excitement and overbid.

Do Your Research

Research market values for vehicles you're interested in. Having an idea of fair market value helps you make informed bids.

Inspect Thoroughly

Take advantage of the inspection period to thoroughly examine vehicles. Don't rush this important step.

Watch & Learn

If it's your first auction, arrive early and observe how the process works before participating.

Bid Clearly

Hold your bidder card high and make clear gestures. Make eye contact with the auctioneer or spotters.

Ask Questions

Our staff is here to help. Don't hesitate to ask questions before or during the auction.

Common Questions About Bidding

Do I need a dealer license to bid?

+

No, our auctions are open to the public. You do not need a dealer license to participate. Both dealers and public buyers bid in the same auction.

Can I bring someone with me to help inspect vehicles?

+

Yes, you're welcome to bring a friend, family member, or mechanic to help you inspect vehicles. They will need to register as a guest upon arrival but do not need to pay the registration fee unless they plan to bid.

What if I can't pay the full amount within 48 hours?

+

Full payment is required within 48 hours. If you cannot meet this requirement, you may forfeit your deposit and the vehicle may be re-auctioned. If you anticipate any issues with payment, please speak with our office staff immediately after the auction.

Are the vehicles guaranteed or warrantied?

+

All vehicles are sold "as-is, where-is" with no warranties or guarantees, either expressed or implied. This is why thorough inspection before bidding is essential.

What fees should I expect besides the winning bid amount?

+

In addition to your winning bid, you'll pay:

  • Buyer's Premium: 5% of the final bid price
  • Documentation Fee: $150
  • Sales Tax (if applicable)
  • Title Transfer Fee: $25

Your registration fee ($25) will be applied toward these fees if you purchase a vehicle.
