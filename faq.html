Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to commonly asked questions about our auctions

When do your auctions take place?

Our regular auctions are held every Monday at 7:00 PM. Doors open at 5:30 PM for registration and vehicle inspection. We also host special themed auctions throughout the year, which are announced on our website and social media.

Where is Ardmore Car Auction located?

We are located at 438 Lodge Rd, Overbrook, OK 73453, just a short drive from downtown Ardmore. Our facility includes a 5-acre lot with ample parking for visitors.

Is Ardmore Car Auction open to the public?

Yes! Our auctions are open to the public, and no dealer license is required to bid. Both dealers and individual buyers are welcome to participate in our auctions.

Can I sell my vehicle at your auction?

Yes, we accept consignments from individuals and dealers. There is a $150 consignment fee, which is refunded if the vehicle sells. To consign a vehicle, please bring your vehicle, title, and ID to our office during business hours (Monday-Friday, 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM) at least 3 days before the auction. For more information, please call us at (580) 226-7710.

How do I register to bid?

Registration is available on auction day from 5:30 PM until the auction begins at 7:00 PM. You'll need to bring a valid government-issued photo ID, proof of address, and a method of payment for the $25 registration fee (refundable with purchase). Once registered, you'll receive a bidder card with your bidder number.

Do I need a dealer license to bid?

No, our auctions are open to the public. You do not need a dealer license to participate. Both dealers and public buyers bid in the same auction.

Can I bring someone with me to help inspect vehicles?

Yes, you're welcome to bring a friend, family member, or mechanic to help you inspect vehicles. They will need to register as a guest upon arrival but do not need to pay the registration fee unless they plan to bid.

How does the bidding process work?

Vehicles are brought into the auction arena one at a time. The auctioneer announces basic details and the starting bid. To place a bid, hold up your bidder card clearly so the auctioneer or spotters can see it. The auctioneer will call out increasing bid amounts, and the highest bidder wins when the auctioneer calls "Sold!" The entire process for each vehicle typically takes about 2-3 minutes.

What happens after I win a bid?

After winning a bid, you'll be directed to the cashier's office where you'll pay a non-refundable deposit ($500 or 10% of the purchase price, whichever is greater), sign a purchase agreement, and receive information about completing payment and vehicle pickup.

What forms of payment do you accept?

We accept the following forms of payment:

  • Cash
  • Credit Cards (subject to a 3% processing fee)
  • Bank Check or Money Order
  • Wire Transfer

Personal checks are not accepted.

When is full payment due?

Full payment is due within 48 hours of the auction. If you cannot meet this requirement, you may forfeit your deposit and the vehicle may be re-auctioned.

What fees should I expect besides the winning bid amount?

In addition to your winning bid, you'll pay:

  • Buyer's Premium: 5% of the final bid price
  • Documentation Fee: $150
  • Sales Tax (if applicable)
  • Title Transfer Fee: $25

Your registration fee ($25) will be applied toward these fees if you purchase a vehicle.

When and how do I pick up my vehicle?

Vehicles must be picked up within 3 business days after full payment. Our office hours for vehicle pickup are Monday-Friday, 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM. You'll need to bring your receipt and ID. If you need to arrange transportation, we can provide recommendations for local towing companies.

Vehicles not picked up after 7 days will incur a storage fee of $25 per day.

How long does it take to receive the title?

For most vehicles, you'll receive the title at the time of pickup after full payment has been made. In some cases involving bank liens or out-of-state titles, there may be a delay of up to 30 days. We'll provide you with a bill of sale and all necessary documentation for temporary registration if needed.

What types of vehicles do you auction?

We auction a wide variety of vehicles including cars, trucks, SUVs, vans, motorcycles, and occasionally RVs and boats. Our inventory comes from various sources including dealer trades, lease returns, bank repossessions, and individual consignments.

Can I inspect vehicles before bidding?

Yes, vehicle inspection is available from 5:30 PM until 6:45 PM on auction day. We encourage all bidders to thoroughly inspect vehicles before bidding. Staff members can start vehicles upon request, but test drives are not available.

Are the vehicles guaranteed or warrantied?

All vehicles are sold "as-is, where-is" with no warranties or guarantees, either expressed or implied. This is why thorough inspection before bidding is essential.

Do you provide vehicle history reports?

For select vehicles, we provide basic vehicle history information. However, we recommend that bidders run their own comprehensive vehicle history reports. We can provide the VIN numbers for vehicles you're interested in during the inspection period.

Can I see what vehicles will be available before auction day?

We post a preliminary inventory list on our Facebook page (facebook.com/ardmorecarauction) by Friday evening before the Monday auction. However, the final inventory may change as additional consignments are accepted up until auction day.
